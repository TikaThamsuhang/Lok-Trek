Lobuche Peak (6,119m)

Accessible Trek & Himalayan Panorama

Lobuche Peak (6,119 m) is an ideal Himalayan summit for trekkers seeking a moderate ascent with spectacular views. Compared to Mera Peak (6,476 m), it is technically easier, featuring gentler slopes and a more accessible route, while still offering impressive Himalayan panoramas. However, Lobuche Peak is slightly less technical than Island Peak (6,189 m), which involves glacier crossings and the use of crampons and ice axes.

This trek passes through glacial valleys and traditional Sherpa villages, providing an authentic cultural immersion. It is an excellent option for trekkers who want to summit an accessible peak while enjoying breathtaking Himalayan scenery. It is also perfect as an acclimatization climb before more technical ascents.

Day by Day Itinerary

Day 1

Arrival in Kathmandu

Arrival in Kathmandu, transfer to hotel, and briefing for the expedition.

Kathmandu Hotel
Day 2

Flight Kathmandu – Lukla (2,840 m) & Trek to Phakding (2,610 m)

A spectacular flight to Lukla, the gateway to the Everest region. After landing, the trek begins gently along the Dudh Koshi River, passing small Sherpa villages and cultivated fields.

3-4 hours Lodge
Day 3

Phakding – Namche Bazaar (3,440 m)

The trek continues up the valley, crossing several suspension bridges over the Dudh Koshi River. After the Sagarmatha National Park checkpoint, the ascent becomes steeper. Arrival in Namche Bazaar, the cultural and economic capital of the Sherpa region.

5-6 hours Lodge
Day 4

Acclimatization Day in Namche Bazaar

A full day dedicated to altitude acclimatization. A hike to nearby villages or viewpoints overlooking the valley is recommended. First views of Everest, Lhotse, and Ama Dablam appear. Afternoon free for rest or village exploration. Overnight in Namche Bazaar.

Acclimatization Lodge
Day 5

Namche Bazaar – Tengboche (3,860 m)

The trail follows a scenic balcony path with magnificent views of the surrounding high peaks. After descending to the river, a final climb leads to Tengboche village. The famous Buddhist monastery overlooks the valley and offers stunning views of Ama Dablam.

5-6 hours Lodge
Day 6

Tengboche – Dingboche (4,410 m)

The trek passes through sparse forests and increasingly mineral landscapes. The altitude becomes noticeable as the trail follows the Imja Khola River to the alpine village of Dingboche.

3-4 hours Lodge
Day 7

Acclimatization Day in Dingboche

An essential acclimatization day with a hike to higher ground above the valley to help the body adapt to altitude. Spectacular views of the surrounding peaks. Return and overnight in Dingboche.

Acclimatization Lodge
Day 8

Dingboche – Lobuche (4,930 m)

The trail enters a high-mountain environment with glacial moraines and rocky terrain. Passing memorials dedicated to climbers lost on Everest. Arrival in the small village of Lobuche marks entry into the Khumbu glacier region.

4-5 hours Lodge
Day 9

Lobuche – Gorak Shep (5,166 m) – Everest Base Camp (5,364 m)

Gradual ascent to Gorak Shep, the last settlement before the glacier. After a break, continue to Everest Base Camp, located on the moraines of the Khumbu Glacier. A grand and historic atmosphere. Return to Gorak Shep for the night.

6-8 hours Lodge
Day 10

Gorak Shep – Lobuche High Camp (5,400 m)

Transition from trekking to climbing. Leaving the main trail, the route ascends to Lobuche High Camp. Camp setup and preparation of technical equipment for the summit attempt.

5-6 hours Tent
Day 11

Summit Lobuche Peak (6,119 m) & Return to High Camp

Very early start for the ascent of Lobuche Peak. The climb includes snow slopes, rocky sections, and roped segments. Sustained but gradual effort. From the summit, enjoy exceptional views of Everest, Lhotse, Nuptse, and Ama Dablam. Careful descent back to High Camp.

10-12 hours Summit 6,119 m Tent
Day 12

High Camp – Lobuche – Dingboche

Gradual descent to more comfortable altitudes. Return to Dingboche allows well-earned recovery after the summit climb.

5-6 hours Lodge
Day 13

Dingboche – Tengboche

Return through the Imja Khola Valley and into rhododendron forests. The landscape becomes greener and more relaxed.

5-6 hours Lodge
Day 14

Tengboche – Namche Bazaar

Descent and short ascents along the Dudh Koshi River before reaching Namche Bazaar. Final views of the high Himalayan peaks.

4-5 hours Lodge
Day 15

Namche Bazaar – Lukla

Continue descending along the same route as the ascent. A relaxed atmosphere with interactions in local villages. Final trekking day to Lukla and celebration with the local team.

5-6 hours Lodge
Day 16

Flight Lukla – Kathmandu

Return flight to Kathmandu. End of the program and free time in the Nepalese capital.

Flight Trip End
