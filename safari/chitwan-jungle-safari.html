Chitwan National Park Safari from Kathmandu – 3 Days / 2 Nights

Discover Chitwan National Park, one of Nepal’s natural treasures and a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

This trip from Kathmandu takes you into the heart of the tropical Terai jungle, where you will encounter exceptional wildlife and the traditional Tharu culture.
An ideal itinerary to complement a trek or a cultural stay in Nepal.

Why Visit Chitwan National Park?

Located in southern Nepal, Chitwan National Park is famous for its remarkable biodiversity. It is home to the one-horned rhinoceros, the Bengal tiger, gharial crocodiles, and more than 500 species of birds.
The park offers an authentic safari experience, combining wild nature, wildlife observation, and cultural encounters.

Detailed Itinerary: Chitwan National Park from Kathmandu

Day 1

Kathmandu – Chitwan National Park

Early morning departure from Kathmandu towards Chitwan National Park. The journey passes through diverse landscapes including green hills, rivers, and traditional Nepali villages.

Upon arrival in Sauraha, the gateway to the park, check in at a lodge or eco-lodge located near the jungle.
In the afternoon, visit a Tharu village, the indigenous people of the Terai region, known for their unique architecture and traditions.

In the evening, enjoy a Tharu cultural dance performance, followed by dinner and an overnight stay at the lodge.

Travel time: 6–8 hours by road (or Kathmandu–Bharatpur flight + transfer)
Day 2

Jungle Safari in Chitwan

After breakfast, depart for a full day of exploration inside Chitwan National Park.

  • Jeep safari (4x4) with a professional naturalist guide: spot rhinoceroses, deer, monkeys, and—with some luck—the Bengal tiger
  • Canoeing on the Rapti River (as per program) to observe crocodiles and aquatic birds
  • Lunch at the lodge or in nature
  • Guided jungle walk or optional activities (elephant breeding center, birdwatching)

Return to the lodge in the late afternoon, followed by dinner and overnight stay.

Day 3

Chitwan – Kathmandu or Pokhara (on request)

Early morning nature walk to enjoy the soft morning light and increased wildlife activity.
After breakfast, return to Kathmandu by road or take a domestic flight from Bharatpur.

Arrival in Kathmandu in the late afternoon or evening.
