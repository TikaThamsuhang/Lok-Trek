Mustang Trek

The Land Beyond the Mountains

The Mustang Trek in Nepal is a unique adventure in the Mustang Valley, a hidden kingdom between the Himalayan peaks and the Tibetan desert. This Mustang trek takes you through authentic Tibetan villages, ancient monasteries, and spectacular lunar landscapes, offering an unparalleled cultural immersion. With breathtaking panoramas, red cliffs, and well-preserved valleys, trekking in the Mustang Valley is perfect for adventure lovers in Nepal, off-the-beaten-path Himalayan trekking, and cultural travel in Mustang.

Guided by local experts, this trek combines safety, comfort, and authentic discovery, making the Mustang Trek an unforgettable experience for all hiking enthusiasts in Nepal and explorers of Himalayan landscapes.

Duration 14 Days
Level Moderate
Group 2 - 12 Pers.
Max Alt. 3,840 m

Day by Day Itinerary

Day 1

Kathmandu - Pokhara (820 m)

Morning flight from Kathmandu to Pokhara to begin your journey toward Mustang. Free day in Pokhara to meet your team, check your equipment, and attend the final briefing. Overnight at the hotel.

Flight Hotel
Day 2

Pokhara - Jomsom (2,720 m) - Kagbeni (2,810 m)

A spectacular flight to Jomsom over the Kali Gandaki Valley between the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges. Upon arrival, an easy walk along the river to Kagbeni, an iconic Tibetan village marking the entrance to the Mustang region. Overnight in a lodge.

Flight ~8 km +90 m Lodge
Day 3

Kagbeni - Chele (3,050 m)

The walk continues north through a semi-arid, lunar landscape bordered by wind-carved cliffs. Passing traditional villages and monasteries before reaching Chele. Overnight in a lodge.

~12 km +240 m Lodge
Day 4

Chele - Syangboche (3,800 m)

Gradual ascent to Syangboche through desert landscapes and rocky formations. This stage reaches higher altitude in Mustang and deepens exposure to northern Tibetan culture. Overnight in a lodge.

~12 km +750 m Lodge
Day 5

Syangboche - Ghami (3,520 m)

The trail winds through arid plateaus and cultivated fields to the traditional village of Ghami, one of the largest Tibetan villages in the region, known for its gompas and sacred chortens. Overnight in a lodge.

~12 km -280 m Lodge
Day 6

Ghami - Tsarang (3,570 m)

Continuation of the trek through the valley to Tsarang, a historic village featuring an ancient monastery and traditional mud-brick houses. Overnight in a lodge.

~11 km +50 m Lodge
Day 7

Tsarang - Lo Manthang (3,840 m)

Ascent to Lo Manthang, the fortified capital of the former Kingdom of Mustang, surrounded by historic walls and dominated by an ancient royal palace. Overnight in a lodge.

~13 km +270 m Lodge
Day 8

Exploration Day in Lo Manthang

Free day to explore the royal palace, monasteries, historic alleys, and nearby villages such as Chhoser, famous for its ancient caves. Overnight in Lo Manthang.

~10 km Lodge
Day 9

Lo Manthang - Drakmar (3,810 m)

Departure from Lo Manthang toward Drakmar, a traditional village with earthen houses and ancient caves carved into the cliffs. Stunning desert panoramas continue throughout the day. Overnight in a lodge.

~16 km -30 m Lodge
Day 10

Drakmar - Ghiling (3,570 m)

Descent through plateaus and valleys to Ghiling, a village surrounded by rock formations and arid plains, offering a peaceful rest stop before continuing the trek. Overnight in a lodge.

~12 km -240 m Lodge
Day 11

Ghiling - Chhusang (3,050 m)

The trek continues south to Chhusang, a strategic village at the entrance of Mustang, known for its colorful cliffs and orchards. Overnight in a lodge.

~13 km -520 m Lodge
Day 12

Chhusang - Jomsom (2,720 m)

Final trekking day to Jomsom along the Kali Gandaki River. A last opportunity to admire snow-capped peaks and deep gorges. Overnight in a lodge.

~14 km -330 m Lodge
Day 13

Jomsom - Pokhara

Morning flight back to Pokhara. Free time for rest, relaxation, or exploration of Phewa Lake. Overnight at the hotel.

Flight Hotel
Day 14

Pokhara - Kathmandu

Transfer by flight or vehicle to Kathmandu. End of the trek.

/ Transfer Trip End
