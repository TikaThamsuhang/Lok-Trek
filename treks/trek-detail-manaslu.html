Manaslu Trek – Direct Crossing of Larkya La Pass

Himalayan Adventure in Nepal

The Manaslu Trek – Direct Crossing of Larkya La Pass is a spectacular and challenging trekking route in Nepal, offering a deep immersion into the heart of the Manaslu Himalayan range (8,163 m). This remote trek passes through traditional Tamang and Gurung villages, alpine forests, and glacial valleys, and includes the crossing of the famous Larkya La Pass (5,160 m), which offers exceptional panoramic views of Manaslu, Himalchuli, and Ganesh Himal.

Trekkers experience authentic local culture, ancient Buddhist monasteries, and well-preserved Himalayan landscapes while staying in mountain lodges along the route. Designed for trekkers in good physical condition, the Manaslu Trek with direct passage of Larkya La combines high altitude, physical challenge, and cultural adventure, delivering an unforgettable experience for mountain and exploration enthusiasts in Nepal.

Day by Day Itinerary

Day 1

Kathmandu → Machha Khola (870 m)

Early morning departure from Kathmandu by vehicle, following the Budhi Gandaki Valley. The road passes through hills, terraced fields, and traditional villages before reaching Machha Khola, the starting point of the trek. Overnight in a lodge.

Drive Lodge
Day 2

Machha Khola → Jagat (1,340 m)

The trek begins along the Budhi Gandaki River, with ascents and descents through narrow gorges, waterfalls, and small villages. Arrival at Jagat, a fortified village marking the official entrance to the Manaslu region. Overnight in a lodge.

~15 km +1,050 m / -650 m Lodge
Day 3

Jagat → Deng (1,860 m)

The trail continues into a more mountainous environment, passing villages with Tibetan-style architecture and wild landscapes. Trek via Philim before reaching Deng. Overnight in a lodge.

~14 km +600 m Lodge
Day 4

Deng → Namrung (2,630 m)

Gradual ascent through forests and isolated hamlets, with increasingly strong Tibetan cultural influences. Arrival at Namrung, offering beautiful views of Manaslu. Overnight in a lodge.

~15 km +1,200 m / -450 m Lodge
Day 5

Namrung → Lho (3,180 m)

Trek through a more open valley with panoramic views of North Manaslu. Arrival at Lho, dominated by its monastery and impressive glaciated peaks. Overnight in a lodge.

~10 km +550 m Lodge
Day 6

Lho → Samagaon (3,530 m)

A gentle ascent through high-altitude pastures and Tibetan villages to Samagaon, the main village of the region and an important acclimatization stop. Overnight in a lodge.

~8 km +350 m Lodge
Day 7

Acclimatization Day in Samagaon

A full day dedicated to acclimatization, with an optional hike to Manaslu Base Camp or Birendra Lake. Overnight in a lodge.

~6 km +/- 400 m Lodge
Day 8

Samagaon → Samdo (3,875 m)

The trek continues through vast high-mountain landscapes with a gradual ascent to the border village of Samdo. Overnight in a lodge.

~8 km +345 m / -150 m Lodge
Day 9

Samdo → Dharamsala / Larkya Phedi (4,460 m)

Gradual ascent through a rocky alpine environment to Dharamsala, the base camp of the Larkya La Pass. Overnight in a basic lodge.

~6 km +585 m Basic Lodge
Day 10

Dharamsala → Larkya La Pass (5,160 m) → Bimthang (3,720 m)

Early morning departure to cross the Larkya La Pass, the highest point of the trek, offering spectacular views of Himlung Himal, Cheo Himal, and Annapurna II. Long descent to the alpine meadows of Bimthang. Overnight in a lodge.

~14 km +700 m / -1,440 m Lodge
Day 11

Bimthang → Dharapani (2,300 m)

Gradual descent through alpine meadows, pine forests, and Gurung villages to Tilije, where the route joins the Annapurna Circuit trail. Overnight in a lodge.

~12 km -1,420 m Lodge
Day 12

Dharapani → Besisahar → Kathmandu or Pokhara

Follow the Marsyangdi Valley through villages and forests, passing Tal and Jagat. Final walking section or short transfer to Besisahar, then return by jeep and bus to Kathmandu or Pokhara.
