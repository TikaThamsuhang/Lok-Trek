Poon Hill – Annapurna Base Camp Trek

Annapurna Base Camp (ABC) Trek via Poon Hill

The Poon Hill – Annapurna Base Camp Trek is one of Nepal’s most iconic trekking routes, ideal for discovering the spectacular landscapes of the Annapurna region while enjoying a rich cultural experience. This trek combines the famous Poon Hill viewpoint (3,210 m)—renowned for its breathtaking sunrise views over the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges—with the adventure of reaching Annapurna Base Camp (ABC, 4,130 m).

Along the way, trekkers explore traditional Gurung and Magar villages, walk through lush forests and sparkling rivers, and experience authentic Nepalese mountain life. The trek offers stunning Himalayan panoramas, making it perfect for photography lovers and trekking enthusiasts.

Suitable for trekkers with good physical fitness, this itinerary is well organized, safe, and includes comfortable lodge accommodation. The Annapurna Base Camp Trek via Poon Hill is an unforgettable journey that perfectly combines nature, culture, and high mountain adventure, offering a complete Himalayan trekking experience in Nepal.

Duration 11 Days
Level Moderate
Group 2 - 12 Pers.
Max Alt. 4,130 m

Day by Day Itinerary

Day 1

Kathmandu → Pokhara

Travel from Kathmandu to Pokhara by tourist bus (approx. 7–8 hours). In the evening, enjoy a relaxing walk around Phewa Lake. Overnight in a hotel.

Drive Hotel
Day 2

Pokhara → Nayapul → Ulleri (1,960 m)

Drive by taxi or private vehicle from Pokhara to Nayapul (about 1 hour 15 minutes). Begin trekking to Ulleri via a steep stone staircase. Overnight in a lodge.

4–5 hours Lodge
Day 3

Ulleri → Ghorepani (2,860 m)

Trek through beautiful rhododendron forests to Ghorepani. If weather and physical condition allow, hike to Poon Hill in the late afternoon for sunset views over the Annapurna and Dhaulagiri ranges. Return to the lodge for dinner. Overnight in a lodge.

4–5 hours Lodge
Day 4

Ghorepani → Poon Hill → Tadapani (2,630 m)

Early morning hike to Poon Hill (45–60 minutes) to witness a spectacular sunrise. From this viewpoint, you can see three 8,000-meter peaks: Dhaulagiri (8,167 m), Manaslu (8,163 m), and Annapurna I (8,091 m), along with Annapurna II, III, IV, Gangapurna, Machhapuchhre (Fishtail), Lamjung Himal, Nilgiri range, Annapurna South, Hiunchuli, and more. After returning to the lodge for breakfast, trek to Tadapani. Overnight in a lodge.

6–7 hours Lodge
Day 5

Tadapani → Sinuwa (2,360 m)

Enjoy beautiful morning views of Annapurna South, Hiunchuli, and Machhapuchhre, then trek through forests and villages to Sinuwa. Overnight in a lodge.

5–6 hours Lodge
Day 6

Sinuwa → Deurali (3,200 m)

Continue trekking through dense forests and alpine landscapes toward Deurali. Overnight in a lodge.

5–6 hours Lodge
Day 7

Deurali → Annapurna Base Camp (4,130 m)

Trek to Annapurna Base Camp, surrounded by towering Himalayan peaks. Enjoy the magnificent sunset views from ABC. Overnight in a lodge.

5–6 hours Lodge
Day 8

Annapurna Base Camp → Bamboo (2,310 m)

Wake up early to experience a stunning panoramic sunrise over Annapurna I, Annapurna South, Machhapuchhre, Hiunchuli, Gangapurna, Annapurna III, and many other peaks. After breakfast, descend to Bamboo. Overnight in a lodge.

6–7 hours Lodge
Day 9

Bamboo → Jhinu Danda (1,780 m)

Trek down to Jhinu Danda, where you can relax in the natural hot springs after the walk. Overnight in a lodge.

5–6 hours Lodge
Day 10

Jhinu Danda → Ghandruk (1,940 m)

Trek to Ghandruk, one of the most beautiful Gurung villages, known for its traditional houses and mountain views. Overnight in a lodge.

Walk Lodge
Day 11

Ghandruk → Pokhara

Trek for about 2 hours to the roadhead, then drive to Pokhara (approximately 1.5 hours). End of the trek.

~2 hours Drive
