Pikey Peak Trek

Trekking in Nepal with Everest Views

The Pikey Peak Trek is a spectacular Himalayan trek in Nepal, located in the Solukhumbu region, offering panoramic views of iconic Himalayan peaks, including Mount Everest, Kanchenjunga, and Manaslu. With a summit altitude of approximately 4,065 m, this trek combines accessibility with cultural immersion, making it ideal for beginners and moderate trekkers.

Over a 7-day itinerary, including road transport from Kathmandu to Dhap, travelers explore traditional Sherpa villages, Buddhist monasteries, and colorful rhododendron forests (especially beautiful in spring). Less crowded than the famous Everest Base Camp trek, the Pikey Peak Trek offers a peaceful adventure, spectacular Himalayan sunrises, and a genuine immersion into Nepalese culture and nature.

Duration 7 Days
Level Moderate
Group 2 - 12 Pers.

Day by Day Itinerary

Day 1

Kathmandu → Dhap (2,850 m)

Early morning departure from Kathmandu by vehicle to Dhap, passing through green hills, Sherpa villages, and rural landscapes of the Solu region. Arrival in the late afternoon at the starting point of the trek. Overnight in a lodge.

Drive Lodge
Day 2

Dhap → Jhapre (2,920 m)

The trek begins with a gentle ascent through pine and rhododendron forests, offering beautiful views of the Solu hills. Arrival at the Sherpa village of Jhapre, known for its panoramic views of Numbur Himal. Overnight in a lodge.

~7 km +300 m / -230 m Lodge
Day 3

Jhapre → Pikey Peak Base Camp (3,640 m)

Gradual ascent along ridges with wide panoramic views of the Everest and Makalu ranges in clear weather. Arrival at Pikey Peak Base Camp, located in a high-altitude pastoral setting. Overnight in a lodge or simple camp.

~9 km +720 m Lodge/Camp
Day 4

Pikey Peak Base Camp → Pikey Peak (4,065 m) → Junbesi (2,700 m)

Very early departure for the ascent of Pikey Peak, the highest point of the trek, offering one of the finest panoramic views in Nepal, including Everest, Lhotse, Makalu, Kanchenjunga, and Annapurna. After sunrise, a long descent to the Junbesi Valley. Overnight in a lodge.

~14 km +425 m / -1,365 m Lodge
Day 5

Junbesi → Phaplu (2,470 m)

The trek continues through Sherpa villages, cultivated fields, and rhododendron forests to Phaplu, the administrative center of the Solu region. Overnight in a lodge.

~11 km +350 m / -580 m Lodge
Day 6

Phaplu → Salleri (2,390 m)

A short and relaxed stage allowing time to fully enjoy the local atmosphere and rural landscapes of Solu. Arrival at Salleri, the district headquarters. Overnight in a lodge.

~6 km +200 m / -280 m Lodge
Day 7

Salleri → Kathmandu

Return by vehicle to Kathmandu via a scenic mountain road with beautiful views of the Nepalese hills. Hotel check-in. End of the trek.

Drive Hotel or Own arrangement
