Three Passes Trek

Everest Three High Passes | Nepal Trek

The Three Passes Trek, also known as the Everest Three High Passes Trek, is one of the most spectacular and challenging trekking routes in Nepal. This high-altitude adventure crosses the three iconic passes of the Khumbu region — Renjo La (5,360 m), Cho La (5,420 m), and Kongma La (5,535 m) — offering breathtaking panoramic views of Mount Everest, Lhotse, Makalu, Cho Oyu, and Ama Dablam.

Duration 18 Days
Level Challenging
Group 2 - 12 Pers.
Max Alt. 5,545 m

The itinerary combines Everest Base Camp, the turquoise Gokyo Lakes, and traditional Sherpa villages, creating a complete Himalayan experience that blends adventure, high-mountain trekking, and cultural immersion. Designed for experienced trekkers in excellent physical condition, this trek offers comfortable lodges, safe organization, and some of the most dramatic landscapes in the Himalayas, making the Three Passes Trek a must-do expedition in Nepal.

Day by Day Itinerary

Route Direction: Renjo La → Cho La → Kongma La

Day 1

Arrival in Kathmandu (1,350 m)

Arrival at Kathmandu International Airport, welcome and transfer to the hotel in Thamel. The day is dedicated to rest, trek preparation, and a briefing with the local team. Overnight at the hotel.

0 km 0 m Hotel
Day 2

Flight Kathmandu – Lukla (2,840 m) & Trek to Phakding (2,650 m)

Early morning flight to Lukla, the gateway to the Khumbu region. The trek begins with an easy walk along the Dudh Koshi River through Sherpa villages to Phakding. Overnight in a lodge.

~8 km 3–4 hrs +350 m / −400 m
Day 3

Phakding to Namche Bazaar (3,440 m)

The trail climbs through pine forests and suspension bridges, entering Sagarmatha National Park. After a steady ascent, arrive at Namche Bazaar, the economic capital of the Khumbu region. Overnight in a lodge.

~11 km 4–5 hrs +900 m / −200 m
Day 4

Acclimatization Day in Namche Bazaar

Acclimatization hike to Khumjung and Khunde villages or the Syangboche viewpoint. Return to Namche Bazaar for a second night.

~5 km 4–5 hrs +600 m / −600 m
Day 5

Namche Bazaar to Thame (3,800 m)

Leaving the classic Everest route, the trail follows the Bhote Koshi Valley toward Thame, a traditional Sherpa village famous for its monastery. Overnight in a lodge.

~9 km 4–5 hrs +650 m / −300 m
Day 6

Thame to Lungden (4,380 m)

The trek continues through an increasingly wild and remote valley with a gradual ascent that supports acclimatization. Arrival in Lungden, the last settlement before Renjo La Pass. Overnight in a lodge.

~7 km 4–5 hrs +650 m
Day 7

Lungden – Renjo La Pass (5,360 m) – Gokyo (4,790 m)

Early ascent to Renjo La Pass, offering spectacular views of Everest, Lhotse, and the Gokyo Lakes. Descend to the famous turquoise lakes of Gokyo. Overnight in a lodge.

~10 km 5–6 hrs +1,100 m / −650 m
Day 8

Gokyo Ri (5,357 m) & Gokyo Lakes

Optional early morning ascent of Gokyo Ri for exceptional panoramic views of Everest, Cho Oyu, and Makalu. The remainder of the day is reserved for rest and acclimatization. Overnight in Gokyo.

~6 km ~4 hrs +850 m / −850 m
Day 9

Gokyo to Thangnak (4,700 m)

Easy walk along the Ngozumpa Glacier, the longest glacier in Nepal. This short stage prepares trekkers for the Cho La crossing. Overnight in a lodge at Thangnak.

~4 km ~2 hrs +250 m / −200 m
Day 10

Thangnak – Cho La Pass (5,420 m) – Dzongla (4,830 m)

Steady ascent, followed by a steeper climb to Cho La Pass, sometimes requiring hands-on scrambling depending on conditions. After crossing the pass, descend to Dzongla. Overnight in a lodge.

~8 km 5–6 hrs +600 m / −500 m
Day 11

Dzongla to Lobuche (4,940 m)

Cross high-altitude terrain dominated by the massive faces of Nuptse and Lobuche Peak. Arrival in Lobuche in the afternoon. Overnight in a lodge.

~6 km 2–3 hrs +250 m / −150 m
Day 12

Lobuche – Gorak Shep (5,170 m) – Everest Base Camp (5,364 m)

Gradual ascent to Gorak Shep, followed by a hike to Everest Base Camp, crossing the Khumbu Glacier moraine. Return to Gorak Shep for overnight stay.

~8 km 3 hrs + 4 hrs +500 m / −200 m
Day 13

Gorak Shep – Kala Patthar (5,545 m) – Lobuche

Early morning ascent of Kala Patthar to witness sunrise over Mount Everest, followed by descent to Lobuche. Overnight in a lodge.

~15 km 5–6 hrs +600 m / −750 m
Day 14

Lobuche – Kongma La Pass (5,535 m) – Chhukung (4,730 m)

The final major challenge of the trek, crossing Kongma La Pass, the wildest and often the most demanding of the three passes. Descend to Chhukung. Overnight in a lodge.

~11 km 6–7 hrs +650 m / −900 m
Day 15

Chhukung – Tengboche (3,885 m)

Gradual descent through the Imja Khola Valley with stunning views of Ama Dablam. Overnight in a lodge.

~9 km 5–6 hrs +150 m / −950 m
Day 16

Tengboche to Namche Bazaar

Return to Namche Bazaar through rhododendron forests and Sherpa villages. Overnight in a lodge.

~14 km 5–6 hrs +400 m / −950 m
Day 17

Namche Bazaar to Lukla

Final trekking day back to Lukla, marking the end of the Khumbu adventure. Overnight in a lodge.

~18 km 4–5 hrs +450 m / −650 m
Day 18

Flight Lukla to Kathmandu

Return flight to Kathmandu and transfer to the hotel. Free time in the city.
