Mera Peak (6,476m)

Himalayan Adventure & Summit | Ideal Peak for Beginners

Mera Peak (6,476 m) is one of the most accessible trekking peaks in Nepal, ideal for trekkers and climbers seeking a true high-mountain experience with a summit above 6,000 meters. Compared to Island Peak, Mera Peak is generally considered technically easier, with gentler slopes and a less exposed route. However, due to its altitude and the length of the trek, it is slightly more demanding than Lobuche Peak.

The ascent offers spectacular views of Everest, Lhotse, Makalu, Cho Oyu, and Kanchenjunga, while passing through glacial valleys and traditional Sherpa villages. This trek requires good physical fitness and gradual acclimatization, but it is an unforgettable experience for trekking, adventure, and Himalayan panorama enthusiasts.

Day by Day Itinerary

Day 1

Kathmandu - Lukla (2,860 m) - Chutanga (3,020 m)

A scenic flight to Lukla, the gateway to the Everest region. The trek begins through pine and rhododendron forests, away from the crowded trails. Overnight at Chutanga in a peaceful natural setting.

4-5 hours Lodge
Day 2

Chutanga - Thuli Kharka (3,900 m) via Chatra La Pass (4,610 m)

Gradual ascent to the Chatra La Pass with first views of the Himalayan peaks. A demanding but scenic day, followed by a short descent to Thuli Kharka.

6-7 hours 4,610 m pass Lodge
Day 3

Thuli Kharka - Kothe (3,600 m)

Descent through forests and remote valleys to the Hinku River. Kothe is a quiet village, perfect for recovery after the first days at altitude.

6-7 hours Lodge
Day 4

Kothe - Thangnak (4,358 m)

Steady ascent through the glacial valley. The landscape becomes more alpine, with views of Mera Peak and surrounding mountains. Overnight at Thangnak near the glacier.

6-7 hours Lodge
Day 5

Thangnak - Khare (5,045 m)

Final approach to Mera Peak Base Camp. The trail follows the glacier in a dramatic high-mountain environment. Overnight in Khare.

5-6 hours Lodge
Day 6

Acclimatization Day at Khare (5,045 m)

Activities include rest, acclimatization walks, and technical training. Instruction on using mountaineering equipment such as crampons, ice axe, and rope.

Acclimatization Lodge
Day 7

Khare - Mera High Camp (5,780 m)

Glacier ascent using crampons to reach High Camp, located on a panoramic ridge with exceptional views of Everest, Makalu, and Lhotse.

4-5 hours Tent
Day 8

Summit Mera Peak (6,476 m) - Return to Khare

Early departure to reach the summit at sunrise. Enjoy a 360° panorama of five 8,000-meter peaks. Long descent back to Khare.

9-10 hours Summit 6,476 m Lodge
Day 9

Khare - Kothe (3,600 m)

Comfortable descent with improved oxygen levels. Return to greener landscapes and warmer temperatures.

5-6 hours Lodge
Day 10

Kothe - Thuli Kharka (3,900 m)

Gradual ascent through the valley with beautiful mountain views. Final night at altitude before returning to Lukla.

5-6 hours Lodge
Day 11

Thuli Kharka - Lukla (2,680 m)

Cross the pass and descend steadily to Lukla. End of the trek and final night in the mountains.

6-7 hours Lodge
Day 12

Lukla - Kathmandu

Return flight to Kathmandu, carrying unforgettable Himalayan memories.

Flight Trip End
